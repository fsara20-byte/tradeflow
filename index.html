Tokenisation layer

Each token represents an economic interest in a verified receivable.

TradeFlow tokenises the receivable’s cash-flow claim, not a speculative crypto asset. The underlying invoice is confirmed, assigned to an SPV, graded, funded by investors, and repaid when the buyer pays the SPV collection account.

1

Legal assignment

The receivable is transferred into a bankruptcy-remote SPV with buyer confirmation and payment redirection.

2

Fractional access

AED 1M invoice can be split into smaller investment units, allowing qualified investors to participate by ticket size.

3

Controlled settlement

Repayments are allocated to token holders by ownership share. Future secondary trading can transfer exposure before maturity.

AI credit assessment

Every receivable receives a transparent credit grade before listing.

Scores combine buyer confirmation, payment history, financial strength, invoice documentation quality, concentration limits, sector risk, sanctions screening and monitoring signals.

Later-stage product

Secondary trading of invoice tokens after platform scale.

Once marketplace depth exists, TradeFlow can introduce controlled secondary trading so investors can exit before maturity and new investors can buy remaining cash-flow exposure at market-cleared pricing.

Primary marketplace

Investors fund newly verified receivables at origination. This is the launch product.

Secondary invoice trading

Investors trade existing tokenised receivable positions before maturity, subject to eligibility and liquidity controls.

Portfolio pools

Institutions and banks access diversified receivable pools by grade, tenor, buyer type and insurance coverage.

Controls

Risk controls built into origination, scoring and settlement.

Fraud control

Buyer confirmation, duplicate invoice checks, legal assignment, document validation and payment redirection to SPV.

Credit control

AI grade, buyer limits, sector limits, reserve fund, insurance on selected receivables and daily monitoring.

Investor protection

KYC, suitability assessment, deal disclosure, token register, collection account controls and ring-fenced receivables.
